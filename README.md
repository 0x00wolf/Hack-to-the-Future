# Hack to the Future



This repository contains information for the Hack to the Future hackathon. Keep calm and hack on!

## Evaluation Criteria

**Project Theme:** TBA

**Core Philosophy for Judging:** Innovation first, working prototypes rewarded, but ideas still competitive.

**Instructions:** Judges will provide a score out of 10 for each of the 4 categories on the provided rubric. We will apply the weighting after judges complete their evaluation in accordance with the weights indicated below.

| Category | Weight | Description & Guiding Questions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | 
| **1. Innovation & Novelty** | **45%** | **The Big Idea: How fresh, original, and creative is the concept?** <br> • **Conceptual Originality:** Is this a novel solution or a unique twist? Does it identify a problem others have missed? <br> • **Human-Centric Creativity:** Does the idea reflect genuine human insight and lived experience? Ideas that appear to be generated by AI and lack personal depth will be penalized. <br> • **Future Potential:** Does this idea have legs? Could it grow into something meaningful with more time? <br><br> *Note: A team with slides only can still score highly here if their idea is brilliant and original.* | **/45** |
| **2. Viability & Execution Path** | **25%** | **The Plan: How well does the team understand what it would take to build this?** <br> • **Scope Awareness:** Is the idea appropriately scoped for a 6-hour hackathon (realistic) or wildly overambitious? <br> • **Technical Clarity:** Does the team have a clear vision of the tech stack, APIs, or tools needed to build their solution? <br> • **MVP Definition:** Can they articulate what a minimum viable product would look like and how their current prototype/demo fits into that vision? | **/25** |
| **3. Prototype Completion** | **15%** | **The Build: How much did they actually accomplish in 6 hours?** <br> • **Tangible Output:** Did they produce *something* functional, even if small? A working button, a script that runs, a Figma flow that connects? <br> • **Fidelity:** How polished is their prototype relative to the time constraint? <br><br> *Note: A team with no code but excellent wireframes can still score moderately here if their design is well thought out.* | **/15** |
| **4. Presentation & Pitch** | **15%** | **The Story: How effectively did the team communicate their idea and demo?** <br> • **Problem/Solution Clarity:** Can they clearly explain the problem and their innovative solution in 3-4 minutes? <br> • **Demo Effectiveness:** Did they show what they built (even if it's a Figma walkthrough or a terminal output)? <br> • **Passion & Enthusiasm:** Does the team seem excited about their idea? Can they speak to it with genuine ownership? | **/15** |

---

### ⚠️ Important: Originality & AI Usage

We will be running the hackathon problem statement through Claude, DeepSeek, and ChatGPT prior to judging. Ideas that closely match AI-generated outputs and are executed along the lines outlined by these LLMs **will lose points** in the Innovation category. We're here to celebrate **human creativity and insight**—bring your own unique perspective!

---

### How Scoring Works

- **Base Score:** Each category is scored 1-10, then multiplied by its weight (e.g., Innovation score of 8 = 36 points toward the total).
